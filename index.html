Elisa Nguyen

elisa.nguyen@live.de

I am passionate about researching how technology can benefit humans and society, in particular the field of responsible and trustworthy AI. I believe that AI offers a great potential to improve our lives and contribute to solving issues but the implementation must ensure fairness and transparency.

Recently, I have started my PhD studies in this direction in the Scalable Trustworthy AI group of Seong Joon Oh at the University of Tübingen, as part of the doctoral program of the International Max Planck Research School for Intelligent Systems (IMPRS-IS). My research is focused on studying methods to understand a model's behavior and build models that can explain their behavior to humans.

Interests

There's many things I like to do, and I was one of the people that picked up a few new hobbies during the pandemic (guilty of buying a sewing machine...) :). In the summer months, I love spending time outside hiking, reading a book somewhere or just having an ice cream with a friend.
